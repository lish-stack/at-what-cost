Summary

Ahold Delhaize (Stop 'n Shop, Giant, Food Lion, Hannaford) scores poorly across all metrics due to systematic rollback of animal welfare commitments and lack of transparency about their supply chain impacts.

Welfare

☆☆☆☆☆ 0/5
4.2 million hens remain in battery cages after Ahold abandoned their 2025 cage-free promise. Each bird gets less space than an iPad for their entire life. The company also delayed eliminating gestation crates for pigs, keeping pregnant sows in stalls where they cannot turn around for four months at a time. No meaningful welfare improvements have been implemented since 2020.

Planet

★☆☆☆☆ 1/5
Their intensive confinement suppliers generate 847 additional tons of CO2 monthly compared to higher-welfare systems. Concentrated animal feeding operations (CAFOs) in their supply chain pollute groundwater in 18 states. The company provides no data on water usage, emissions by facility, or manure management practices. They've eliminated previous environmental impact reports while increasing sourcing from factory farms.

Trust

☆☆☆☆☆ 0/5
Ahold quietly deleted their cage-free commitment from websites and reports without announcement. This follows broken promises on gestation crates (2022) and sustainable seafood (2023). They spend millions marketing "responsible sourcing" while systematically weakening standards. When questioned, they cite vague "supply chain challenges" that competitors have overcome.
