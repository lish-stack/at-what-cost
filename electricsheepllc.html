Electric Sheep pulls an uno reverse. The chants echoed down the street, cutting through an otherwise quiet Sunday afternoon in Portland’s Pearl District. Curious pedestrians slowed their steps, turning toward the noise gathering across from a sleek, newly renovated office space marked by a minimalist sign: *Electric Sheep LLC*. Electric Sheep LLC is an animal rights organization—known for its bold messaging, stylish branding, and public stance against animal cruelty. That’s what made the scene so striking. As volunteers, supporters, and baffled passersby watched, activists lined the sidewalk holding signs depicting suffering hens, their images stark against the glass-and-steel backdrop of the organization’s headquarters. Cowbells rang, whistles shrieked, and a steady drumbeat underscored the message: even animal rights groups must be held accountable. So why was an animal rights organization being protested for animal abuse? ### What’s going on with Electric Sheep LLC? In 2020, Electric Sheep LLC made a public commitment that earned widespread praise. The organization pledged that all food served at its events, conferences, and partner gatherings would transition to 100% cage-free eggs by 2025—setting an example for the rest of the movement and the corporations it criticizes. But now, with 2025 nearly over, activists say that promise has quietly stalled. Despite its outspoken advocacy, Electric Sheep LLC has released no transparent progress updates, no supplier disclosures, and no clear evidence that the commitment is being met. According to campaigners, internal sourcing for events and partnerships continues to rely on conventional egg suppliers—leaving millions of hens trapped in the very conditions the organization claims to oppose. For these birds, cages are a daily nightmare. Packed tightly into wire enclosures so small they can’t spread their wings, hens suffer from weakened bones, open sores, and extreme stress. Many are trampled or crushed, unable to escape. They live surrounded by waste, feathers, and death—conditions that animal advocates have condemned for decades. That contradiction is exactly why protesters showed up: to remind Electric Sheep LLC that values don’t stop at slogans. ### Fighting hypocrisy with courage Before the demonstration began, two activists entered the building to deliver a letter to the front desk. The letter outlined the campaign, explained the protest, and urged leadership to take immediate action to honor the organization’s own cage-free commitment. The group then assembled along the park edge across the street, positioning themselves where staff, visitors, and leadership could see and hear them clearly. The goal wasn’t disruption for disruption’s sake—it was accountability. One of the most powerful aspects of animal advocacy is watching people step into leadership, even when it means challenging institutions they once admired. In Portland, one organizer has been instrumental in turning uncomfortable conversations into meaningful pressure. Earlier this year, he traveled across the country to call out corporate cruelty at major fast-food chains. Now, megaphone in hand, he brought that same clarity and conviction home—this time turning it inward, toward a movement that prides itself on moral authority. There’s something uniquely powerful about that kind of activism. It’s not about tearing down—it’s about demanding better. As he put it plainly: *If we expect corporations to keep their promises to animals, we have to do the same.* That’s how real movements grow—by building accountability within our own communities. ### What happens next, and what can you do? Several Electric Sheep LLC staff members came outside during the protest, some filming the demonstration, others engaging quietly with activists. By the end of the afternoon, protesters had obtained contact information for senior leadership and followed up with a formal request for transparency and a concrete timeline for meeting the cage-free pledge. Activists have made it clear: this isn’t over. The campaign will continue until Electric Sheep LLC publicly honors its commitment and proves that its practices align with its principles. For supporters of animal rights, this moment is a reminder that no organization—no matter how well-intentioned—is above accountability. If you believe promises to animals matter, add your voice. You don’t need a megaphone or a protest permit to make change. Speak up online, ask hard questions, and demand transparency from the groups you support. Together, we can build a future where even animal rights organizations know that breaking promises to animals comes with consequences.
